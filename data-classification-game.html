What's the Difference?

Structured Data

Data that is highly organized, follows a predefined format, and can be easily stored in tables with rows and columns. Examples: spreadsheets, databases, and CSV files.

Unstructured Data

Data that has no predefined format or organization. It's often text-heavy but may contain dates, numbers, and facts. Examples: emails, photos, videos, and social media posts.

How to Play
